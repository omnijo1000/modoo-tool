What is ROI (Return on Investment)?

ROI = (Final Value − Initial Investment − Costs) / (Initial Investment + Costs) × 100. It measures the efficiency of an investment as a percentage. Annualized ROI (CAGR) = (Final/Initial)^(1/years) − 1.

Q. What is a good ROI?
It depends on asset class. Stock market: ~10%/yr historically. Real estate: 8–12%/yr. Savings accounts: 3–5%. Venture capital targets: 20%+. Always compare against benchmarks for the same risk level.

Q. ROI vs CAGR — what is the difference?
ROI is a simple total return, while CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) shows the annualized rate assuming compounding. CAGR is better for comparing investments held for different durations.
