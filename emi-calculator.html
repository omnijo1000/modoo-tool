What is EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. It includes both principal repayment and interest.

Q. How is home loan EMI calculated?
EMI = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ / ((1+r)ⁿ−1), where P = principal, r = monthly rate (annual rate ÷ 12), n = tenure in months. For ₹20L at 8.5% for 20 years: EMI ≈ ₹17,356.

Q. What is a good EMI-to-income ratio?
Lenders prefer total EMIs to be within 40–50% of monthly gross income (FOIR). A ratio under 35% gives you a comfortable cushion. Always calculate before taking a loan.

Q. Home loan vs personal loan EMI difference?
Home loans typically offer lower rates (8–10%) and longer tenure (up to 30 years), resulting in lower EMIs. Personal loans have higher rates (12–24%) and shorter tenure (1–5 years).
