What is Inflation?

Inflation is the rate at which the general level of prices rises over time, eroding purchasing power. $1,000 in 2000 at 3% annual inflation would be worth approximately $2,094 in 2026 — meaning you'd need twice as much money to buy the same things.

Q. How does inflation affect savings?
If your savings earn 2% interest but inflation is 3%, your real return is −1%. Your money grows nominally but loses purchasing power. This is why investing in assets that beat inflation (stocks, real estate) is important.

Q. What's a normal inflation rate?
Most central banks target 2% annual inflation as healthy. Above 5% is considered high; above 10% is a crisis. The US saw 9% inflation in 2022, the highest since 1981.
