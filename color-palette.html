Color Harmony Theory

Complementary: Colors opposite on the color wheel (e.g., blue & orange). High contrast, vibrant.
Triadic: Three colors equally spaced (120° apart). Balanced yet colorful.
Analogous: Colors adjacent on the wheel. Harmonious, natural feel.
Monochromatic: Tints and shades of one hue. Elegant, cohesive.

Q. What is a color palette for?
Color palettes are essential in UI/UX design, brand identity, web development, and graphic design. A well-chosen palette ensures visual harmony and accessibility (sufficient contrast ratios for readability).
