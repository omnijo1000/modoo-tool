What is SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)?

SIP is a method of investing a fixed amount in mutual funds at regular intervals (usually monthly). It leverages rupee-cost averaging and compound interest to build long-term wealth. SIPs are available in equity funds, debt funds, hybrid funds and ELSS (tax saving) funds.

Q. How much should I invest in SIP monthly?
A rule of thumb: invest at least 20% of your monthly income. ₹10,000/month at 12% for 20 years = ₹99.9 lakh. Starting early makes the biggest difference due to compounding.

Q. SIP vs lump sum — which is better?
SIP is better for salaried investors as it averages out market volatility. Lump sum works best when markets are at a low. Many investors combine both: SIP for regular income and lump sum during corrections.

Q. Is SIP income taxable?
Equity SIP gains held over 1 year are taxed at 10% LTCG (above ₹1 lakh). Debt fund gains are taxed as per income slab. ELSS funds offer ₹1.5 lakh deduction under Section 80C.
