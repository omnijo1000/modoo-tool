How to Calculate Mortgage Payment

Monthly P&I = L × r(1+r)ⁿ / ((1+r)ⁿ−1), where L = loan amount, r = monthly rate (annual ÷ 12), n = total payments. Add monthly property tax and insurance for total monthly cost.

Q. How much mortgage can I afford?
The 28% rule: monthly housing costs should not exceed 28% of gross monthly income. For a $6,000/month salary, max payment is $1,680.

Q. How does down payment affect my mortgage?
A larger down payment reduces your loan amount and monthly payment. Putting 20% down typically eliminates PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance), saving $100–200/month.
